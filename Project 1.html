Thosands flocked to New Orleans Lousianna this June to compete in the North America International Championships(NAIC) but one pokemon was sighted far more then others.

By Savanna Rodriguez

The Pokémon TCG, often abbreviated as PTCG, is a tabletop card game based on the Pokémon franchise. PTCG is both collectable and strategic card game where players assume the role of Pokémon Trainers and battle with decks of 60 cards, including Pokémon, energy cards to power attacks, and trainer cards for special effects.

Insights into the most popular decks and strategies that dominate the current competitive scene are often referred to as "Meta" or "Meta Share." These insights often include deck analysis, detailed breakdowns of deck archetypes and matchup data to understand how different decks perform in tournaments. The go to website for this information is Limitless TCG

This year the final International Championships event of the 2026 Championship Series season, North America International Championships(NAIC) was hosted in New Orleans, Louisiana. This two day completive highly competitive event highly favored one deck type.

Dragapult remains one of the most widely played decks in the current Pokémon TCG competitive scene. Druagpults high usage also comes with high amounts of success. The deck is credited with 267 top 8 reginal finishes including 33 wins and 47 international top 8 finishes including 4 wins.

Dragapult is highly favored in this meta since it combines relentless early‑game pressure with exceptional consistency and flexible deck construction. Its ability to deal targeted spread damage using to main attack Phantom Dive which allows it to dismantle evolving boards and punish support Pokémon that many top decks rely on. The archetype’s core engine is remarkably stable, with nearly all essential cards appearing in 97–100% of lists, underscoring how refined and optimized the deck has become.
